Information about the National Museum of the History of Ukraine

The museum began its activity with an archaeological exhibition in 1899 as the Museum of Antiquities and Arts. The first museum department of archeology was headed by the pioneer of Trypil and Chernyakhiv cultures, prominent Ukrainian archaeologist Vikentiy Khvoyka. An extremely important role in the formation of the museum's collection was played by its first director, Mykola Bilyashivskyi. In 1904, the museum was consecrated and solemnly opened under a new name - Kyiv Art, Industrial and Scientific Museum named after Emperor Mykola Oleksandrovych.

The museum was supported by the families of patrons Tereshchenko and Khanenko. They financed archaeological expeditions, contributing to the formation of historical and ethnographic collections. Since the beginning of the First World War, the museum actively continued its work - receiving visitors, completing stock collections. Its structure included archaeological, artistic and industrial, artistic, numismatic, historical, ethnographic departments, the "Old Kyiv" department and a library.

Museum of butterflies, Kyiv

The museum exhibits more than 1,000 specimens of butterflies and other insects from all over the world.

Geological Museum

The Geology Museum preserves, examines, and displays actual scientific rock material that relates to many aspects of the study of planet Earth.

Today, the National Museum of the History of Ukraine is one of the leading museums of Ukraine in terms of the number and value of its collections. Its stock collections include more than 800,000 exhibits. These are world-important archaeological and numismatic collections, ethnographic collections, collections of weapons, monuments of decorative and applied art, manuscripts, old prints, works of painting and graphics, relics of the Ukrainian national liberation movement of the 20th century. They reflect the history of Ukraine from ancient times to the present.
