Information about the Museum of butterflies

The museum exhibits more than 1,000 specimens of butterflies and other insects from all over the world. Lepidoptera (butterflies, moths) are a series of insects with complete metamorphosis. It probably arose at the beginning of the Mesozoic, reliable fossil remains of the series are known from the Cretaceous period. The wings of insects of this order, as well as most of the body, are covered with scales. Lepidoptera are distributed on all continents except Antarctica, and the greatest diversity is reached in the tropics. There are more than 4,000 species in Central Europe. More than 160,000 species have been described in the world fauna.
