Information about the Geological Museum

It was founded in 1834 as the Mineralogical Office of the Department of Mineralogy and Geognosy of the University of St. Volodymyr. The initial fund for it was 15,538 specimens of the Volyn Lyceum, 1,457 specimens of the Lutsk Gymnasium and several hundred exhibits of the Vilnius University. At the beginning of 1837, the collection of the Mineralogical Cabinet already counted 19,362 samples of minerals, rocks and fossils.

The Geology Museum preserves, examines, and displays actual scientific rock material that relates to many aspects of the study of planet Earth. This material testifies to the material composition of the earth's crust, the structure, origin and development of our planet, to the complex phenomena and processes that took place on its surface and in its interior.
